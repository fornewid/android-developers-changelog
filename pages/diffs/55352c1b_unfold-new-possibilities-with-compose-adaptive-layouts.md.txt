 ## Putting adaptive principles into practice
 
 For developers who want to get their apps ready for this adaptive future, here are a few key [best practices](https://android-developers.googleblog.com/2025/05/adaptiveapps-io25.html) to keep in mind:
 
-* **Take inventory:** The first step is to see where you are today. [Test your app](/training/testing/different-screens) on a large screen device or with the resizable emulator in Android Studio to identify areas for improvement, like stretched UIs or usability issues.
-* **Support optimized layouts:** Use libraries like [Compose Adaptive Layouts](/develop/ui/compose/build-adaptive-apps#compose_material_3_adaptive) to build UI that adapts to different window sizes and device postures. Your app should work well in both portrait and landscape, without [restricting orientation](/develop/ui/compose/layouts/adaptive/app-orientation-aspect-ratio-resizability).
-* **Think beyond touch:** A great adaptive experience means supporting all input methods. This goes beyond basic functionality to include thoughtful details that users expect, like hover states for mouse cursors, context menus on right-click, and support for [keyboard shortcuts](/develop/ui/compose/touch-input/keyboard-input/keyboard-shortcuts-helper).
