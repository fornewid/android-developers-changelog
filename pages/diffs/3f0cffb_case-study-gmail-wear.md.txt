 ## Results
 
 As a result of this analysis, the Gmail Wear OS team made the following
 improvements:
 
-* Since they noticed contention during app startup when looking at the [CPU
-  activity](#runnable-state), they replaced the spinner animation used to
-  indicate that the app is loading with a single static image. They also
-  prolonged the splash screen to defer shimmer state, the second screen state
-  used to indicate that the app is loading, to free CPU resources. This improved
-  app startup latency by 50%.
-* From looking at time spent in
-  [`OpenDexFilesFromOat*`](#opendexfilesfromoat) and [JIT
-  activity](#jit-activity), they enabled
-  [R8](/build/shrink-code#shrink-code) rewriting of [Baseline
-  Profiles](/topic/performance/baselineprofiles/create-baselineprofile). This
-  improved app startup latency by 20%.
-
-**Tip:** To enable R8 rewriting of Baseline Profiles, make sure you're using AGP 8.2
-or higher.
-[See the functionalities that come with each version of AGP](/topic/performance/baselineprofiles/overview#recommended-versions).
+- Since they noticed contention during app startup when looking at the [CPU
+  activity](https://developer.android.com/topic/performance/appstartup/case-study-gmail-wear#runnable-state), they replaced the spinner animation used to indicate that the app is loading with a single static image. They also prolonged the splash screen to defer shimmer state, the second screen state used to indicate that the app is loading, to free CPU resources. This improved app startup latency by 50%.

-* Use A/B testing for changes you think will improve things, and reject them if
