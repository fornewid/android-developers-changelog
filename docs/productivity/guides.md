# About productivity apps Stay organized with collections Save and categorize content based on your preferences.




Productivity apps empower users to be more efficient and to unleash their
creativity in areas as diverse as crafting business documents, managing
schedules, creating comics, and designing social media thumbnails. In this
developer center, you'll find resources to help you create, extend, and level up
these types of experiences, making the most of the form factors and input
capabilities of Android devices.

[Level up your productivity app](/productivity/guides/basic-better-best)

## Adaptive layouts

Make the most of devices with [large screens](/develop/ui/compose/layouts/adaptive/support-different-display-sizes), such as tablets, foldables,
and ChromeOS laptops, support [app continuity](/guide/topics/large-screens/learn-about-foldables#app_continuity) on foldables when
transitioning between folded and unfolded states, and make your app fully
[support multi-window modes](/guide/topics/large-screens/multi-window-support).

## Leverage AI and machine learning

[On-device](/ai/aicore) and [cloud-based](/ai/vertex-ai-firebase) generative AI offer differentiating new ways
to improve productivity. On-device models unlock generative AI in a
privacy-preserving way while allowing for features such as text summarization, writing
assistance, sentiment or mood analysis, and more on supported top-tier devices.
Cloud solutions provide broad device support with powerful multimodal
capabilities such as image and video descriptions, working with large documents,
formatted responses and more.

## Work with hardware and accessories

Users can be more productive when using accessories like keyboards, mice,
trackpads, and styluses, so supporting this hardware is key to empowering user
productivity. Make sure to support keyboard navigation ([with views](/develop/ui/views/touch-and-input/keyboard-input/navigation),
[with Compose](/develop/ui/compose/touch-input/focus))), [shortcuts](/develop/ui/compose/touch-input/keyboard-input/keyboard-shortcuts-helper) ([with views](/develop/ui/views/touch-and-input/keyboard-input/commands), [with Compose](/develop/ui/compose/touch-input/keyboard-input/commands))), and
alternative pointing devices such as mouse, trackpad, and stylus
([with views](/develop/ui/views/touch-and-input/input-events?), [with Compose](/develop/ui/compose/touch-input/pointer-input))).

## Work with text and media

Editing text and working with media is a core function of many productivity
apps, and you'll want to support core OS features that users expect, such as copy
and paste ([with views](/develop/ui/views/touch-and-input/copy-paste), [with Compose](/develop/ui/compose/touch-input/copy-and-paste))), browsing user media with
[photo picker](/training/data-storage/shared/photopicker), support for emoji ([with views](/guide/topics/large-screens/learn-about-foldables#app_continuity), [with Compose](/develop/ui/compose/text/emoji))), and
rich content interop with support for drag and drop and image keyboards
([with views](/develop/ui/views/touch-and-input/drag-drop), [with Compose](/develop/ui/compose/touch-input/user-interactions/drag-and-drop))).
